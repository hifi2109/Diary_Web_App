Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Overview

Jesse's Diary is a personal web application. This policy explains what data is accessed, how it is used, and your rights. In short: your data stays in your own Google account and is never shared with anyone.

Data we access

When you sign in with Google, this app requests permission to access:

  • Google Drive (App Data folder) — to save and load your diary entries. This is a private, hidden folder that only this app can see.
  • Google Photos (read-only) — to let you browse and import photos or videos from your Google Photos library into diary entries.

How your data is stored

All diary entries and uploaded media are stored exclusively in your own Google account. This app has no servers, no databases, and no backend. Nothing is ever transmitted to or stored by any third party.

Data sharing

Your data is never sold, shared, or disclosed to any third party. The only services this app communicates with are Google's own APIs, using your personal access token, on your behalf.

Google API usage

This app's use of Google APIs complies with the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Cookies & tracking

This app uses no cookies, analytics, or tracking of any kind.

Revoking access

You can revoke this app's access to your Google account at any time by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, you can reach out to the app's creator directly.
